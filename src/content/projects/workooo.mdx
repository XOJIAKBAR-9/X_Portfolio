## Project Overview

Across every region of Uzbekistan, there are many daily and temporary job opportunities — but no centralized platform to connect workers and employers. Workooo solves this problem by bringing both sides together on a single, easy-to-use platform.

Inspired by the jobs section of Korea's Carrot (당근마켓) app, Workooo is adapted specifically for the Uzbek market and the unique needs of local workers and employers.

## How It Works

**For Workers:**
- Browse job listings filtered by region
- Submit applications directly through the platform
- Communicate with employers through built-in chat once accepted

**For Employers:**
- Post daily or short-term job listings
- Review incoming applications
- Accept or reject candidates
- Rate workers after job completion

## Technical Approach

The platform is built with Django on the backend, using PostgreSQL for data storage. The architecture handles real-time chat between workers and employers, a rating system for building trust, and regional filtering to keep job listings relevant.

Key technical decisions included building a robust application workflow (post → apply → accept/reject → rate) and implementing location-based filtering to help users find opportunities in their area.

## What I Learned

Building Workooo taught me a lot about designing systems that handle real user workflows — from managing application states to implementing chat functionality. It also reinforced the importance of thinking about the user experience from both sides of a marketplace.
